Project Goal: Create a high-fidelity, interactive RSVP landing page for "Srinivas Kalyanam" that feels like a premium, gold-embossed physical invitation.

1. Visual DNA (The "Vibe")
Aesthetic: "Modern Tanjore" meets "Royal Temple Scroll."

Texture: Replace flat colors with Gold Foil Paper texture and Cream Handmade Paper backgrounds.

Key Motif: Use the Gopuram (temple tower) silhouette and Shankha/Chakra (conch/wheel) symbols instead of generic flowers.

The Banana Leaves: Do not use bright green vectors. Use Deep Emerald Green with Metallic Gold Veining. They should look like heavy silk or hand-painted illustrations.

2. Layout & Interaction (The "Magic")
The "Reveal" Hero Section: * Initial State: Two large, detailed banana leaves meet in the center, acting as a "curtain."

Interaction: As the user scrolls, the leaves slide horizontally OUT to the edges, revealing the invitation text as if opening a sacred gate.

The Typography: * Headlines: Cinzel Decorative or Playfair Display (Bold) in a Gold Gradient.

Body: Lora or Crimson Pro for a classic, readable feel.

3. Screen-by-Screen Breakdown
Screen A: The Invitation (The Entry)
Background: Ivory parchment with a 5% opacity "Mandala" watermark.

Top: A small, glowing Gold Om (ॐ) or Venkateswara Tilak.

Center: * "The Guttula Family" (Small, Elegant Serif)

SRINIVAS KALYANAM (Large, tracked out, Gold Foil effect)

Details: Date, Time, and Venue inside a thin, ornate Temple Border.

Screen B: The Interactive RSVP (The Scroll)
Form Design: A floating glassmorphism card with a Gold Border.

Fields: Name (Floating label) and a "Presence" toggle.

The Button: A deep Maroon button with gold text: "Confirm Presence."

Screen C: The Response (The Thank You)
If YES: The background transitions to a celebratory Saffron/Maroon gradient. Confetti of Marigold Petals falls from the top.

Text: "Dhanyavaadamulu! Your presence will grace this divine occasion."

Action: A prominent "Add to Calendar" button styled like a gold coin.

If NO: A soft, respectful cream screen.

Text: "We will miss your presence. May Lord Venkateswara’s blessings be with you."

Action: A small "Update Response" link in case they change their mind.
